How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.
What is the oldest U.S. national park in the system?
Yellowstone National Park (in Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana) is the oldest U.S. national park, founded in 1872.
What is the largest national park in the system?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park, Alaska, is the largest national park, covering 13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.
What is the largest national park in the lower 48?
Death Valley National Park is the largest national park in the lower 48 U.S. states, stretching 5,300 square miles or 3.4 million acres.
What is the smallest national park in the system?
The smallest national park is Hot Springs National Park in Arkansas, coming in at about 5,500 acres. This was actually the first federally protected piece of land in 1832. It was not named a national park until 1921.
What park gets the most visitors?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park is the most visited national park with more than 9 million guests per year, followed by Grand Canyon National Park, which gets more than 4 million visits per year.
What park gets the least visitors?
Alaska is home to one of the least visited national parks: Kobuk Valley National Park receives just 3,000 visitors per year.
Are the parks just open wilderness and forests?
Not so - for example, national parks are home to a former federal prison, Alcatraz in Golden Gate National Recreation Area, California, and a leper colony, Molokai Island Hansen's Disease Settlement in Kalaupapa National Historic Site, Hawaii.

What is the most remote park in the system?
Located 70 miles from Key West in the Gulf of Mexico, Dry Tortugas National Park is a collection of seven tiny islands that can be reached only by boat or plane, making it the country's most remote national park.

San Antonio Missions

  • All four churches within the park are active Catholic parishes with active services in English, Spanish, or both!
  • The Alamo is actually not part of the park and is administered separately.
  • The Missions were designated a UNESCO Heritage Site in 2015.
  • The San Antonio Missions received 614,810 visitors in 2012.

Big Bend

  • There is a legal and active border crossing in the park between Mexico and the United States.
  • The park was authorized by Congress in 1935 to preserve and protect an area of the Chihuahuan Desert along the Rio Grande.
  • There are numerous fossils from the Cretaceous and Tertiary periods that are studied.
  • The park features desert and rugged hills/mountain areas and is over 800,000 acres.

Big Thicket

  • Big Thicket is a preserve vs a park meaning that, with a permit, you can consume the resources in the area whether hunting, trapping, or otherwise.
  • Carnivorous Plants can be found on some of the trails including pitcher plants and sundews.
  • There are no developed campgrounds or campsites designated, but with a permit you can camp in the wilds therein.
  • Prior to human expansion and development, black bears were common in the area but are now limited to rare sightings of individual bears or small populations wandering over from Louisiana.
